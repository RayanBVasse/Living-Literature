About

About Rayan B. Vasse

Rayan B. Vasse is the pen name of an independent researcher and author holding a PhD.

His work explores how people remain coherent across changing roles, cultures, emotional states, and technological environments, and how books, dialogue, and narrative archives can become tools for understanding that change across time.

Background

With a background spanning science, systems thinking, and long-term cross-cultural observation, Rayan's work moves between literary reflection, conceptual theory, and methodological research.

Some parts of that work take the form of books. Others become papers, essays, or applied systems. Together, they form a broader inquiry into personhood in the modern age.

This Ecosystem

Living Literature is part of that wider inquiry.

The ecosystem currently brings together:

Current Structure

  • a multi-volume book series on identity, belonging, emotion, and reflective life
  • research in longitudinal text analysis and narrative change
  • applied systems exploring reading, dialogue, compatibility, and narrative infrastructure
  • public essays written from inside the evolving system

Common Thread

Identity is not a snapshot. It unfolds across time, and language is one of the places where that unfolding becomes visible.

A Note on Method

This work moves across genres and disciplines. Some writing is literary and reflective. Some is conceptual. Some is methodological and research-facing. The variation is deliberate.

Rather than separating books, essays, systems, and research into unrelated silos, this platform treats them as connected forms of inquiry into how people make meaning, sustain continuity, and change across time.

Note on indices

Building these indices required a specific and unusual combination: scientific training (to understand what validation, methodology, and a real index actually means), long-form lived experience across cultures and professional environments (to have the material to build from), and systems thinking (to design something that works as an interconnected whole rather than isolated tools). A clinical psychologist alone couldn't have built this. A technologist alone couldn't have built this. A writer alone couldn't have built this.

The simplification throughout this series is not a compromise. It is the innovation. The ideas in academic psychology and identity theory are real, well-evidenced, and important - and almost entirely inaccessible to the people who need them most. Smudged Edges of Self is an attempt to change that.

A note on authorship

Rayan B. Vasse is a pen name adopted for this body of work. The author's PhD was awarded by Stockholm University, Sweden.

The books, essays, and reflective tools published under this name are independent intellectual works. They do not represent the clinical or professional practice of the author's credentialled field.

The Living Literature system is the subject of a UK patent application filed November 2025 (ref. LL-PAT-2025).
